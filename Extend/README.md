# VesSAP Cross-Domain Extension

## Overview

This extension evaluates whether the FullAttentionUNet trained on micro-CT mouse vessel data
can generalise to a fundamentally different imaging modality — VesSAP light-sheet fluorescence
microscopy. The core question is: **how much does domain shift degrade performance, and can
fine-tuning on a small VesSAP dataset recover it?**

## Data Extension & Generalization Test

### Source
To evaluate the generalization ability of our Network, we extended the testing from the training domain (**MiniVess**) to an independent dataset (**VesSAP**). Both datasets represent rodent cerebrovasculature but differ significantly in their physical imaging principles and data characteristics:

| Property | MiniVess (Baseline Training) | VesSAP (Cross-Domain Extension) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Imaging Modality** | **2-Photon Fluorescence (2PFM)** | **Light-Sheet Fluorescence (LSFM)** |
| **Contrast Mechanism** | Point-scanning excitation | Selective plane (sheet) illumination |
| **Biological State** | *In vivo* (Live animal) | *Ex vivo* (Tissue clearing / vDISCO) |
| **Channels** | Single (e.g., FITC-dextran) | Dual (**WGA** for capillaries + **EB** for large vessels) |
| **Resolution ($x,y,z$)** | **Sub-micron** (~0.5 $\mu m$ isotropic) | **Voxel-level** (~3.0 $\mu m$ isotropic) |
| **Noise Profile** | Poisson noise & Motion artifacts | Striping artifacts & Scattering |
| **Dataset Scale** | Small localized stacks | **Whole-brain** volumetric scans |


### VesSAP Dual-Channel Design
VesSAP uses two complementary fluorescent dyes to label vessels of different sizes:

- **ch0 — WGA (Wheat Germ Agglutinin)**: binds to vessel walls, produces strong signal
  in small capillaries and fine vessel networks. Weaker in large vessels.
- **ch1 — EB (Evans Blue)**: fills vessel lumen via blood flow, produces strong signal
  in large vessels and arteries. Weaker in capillaries.

The two channels are **complementary** — neither alone captures the full vascular tree.

## Preprocessing

### Channel Fusion Strategies
Since the pretrained model expects single-channel input, the two VesSAP channels must be
fused. Two strategies are evaluated:

**ch0_only** — Use only the WGA channel.
Rationale: micro-CT also produces a single greyscale channel where vessels appear as bright
structures against dark background, similar to WGA fluorescence. This strategy minimises the
input distribution shift.

**max_fusion** — Pixel-wise maximum of ch0 and ch1.
Rationale: retains the strongest signal from either dye at every voxel, theoretically
capturing all vessel sizes. Formula: `fused[i,j,k] = max(ch0[i,j,k], ch1[i,j,k])`.

### Normalisation Pipeline
1. **Percentile clipping** — clip intensities to [p0.5, p99.5] to remove outliers
2. **Min-max rescaling** — rescale to [0, 1]

### Patch Extraction
For training (Exp B and C), volumes are divided into overlapping 3D patches:
- **Patch size**: 64 × 64 × 64 voxels
- **Overlap**: 25% between adjacent patches
- **Filtering**: patches with vessel ratio < 0.1% are discarded (background-only patches
  provide no useful gradient signal)
- **Yield**: sample_1 → 121 patches, sample_2 → 106 patches

### Cross-Validation Split
With only 2 samples, **leave-one-out (LOO)** cross-validation is used:

| Fold | Train | Test |
|------|-------|------|
| fold_1 | sample_2 | sample_1 |
| fold_2 | sample_1 | sample_2 |

## Experiment Design

### Pretrained Model
All experiments use `../runs_final/fold0/checkpoints/best.pth` as the pretrained model.
This is the **FullAttentionUNet** (Attention Gate + Inter-slice Attention) trained on all
43 accepted micro-CT volumes, achieving best validation Dice of **0.8820** at epoch 25.

Architecture: `FullAttentionUNet(in_channels=3, out_channels=1, base_channels=64)` —
36.1M parameters.

### 2.5D Adaptation for 3D VesSAP Data
The pretrained model is 2D but VesSAP data is 3D. To bridge this gap, **2.5D slice-by-slice
inference** is used — identical to the training strategy:

- For each axial slice `t`, the input is `[slice_{t-1}, slice_{t}, slice_{t+1}]` stacked as
  3 channels
- Boundary slices use reflect padding
- This preserves the exact input format the model was trained on

During training (Exp B, C), 3D patches `(B, 1, D, H, W)` are automatically decomposed into
2.5D slice batches `(B×D, 3, H, W)` via the `patch_to_slices()` function.

---

### Experiment A — Direct Inference (No Fine-tuning)

**Purpose**: Quantify the raw domain gap between micro-CT and VesSAP without any adaptation.

**Protocol**: Load pretrained weights → run 2.5D inference on each test volume → search for
optimal threshold in [0.1, 0.9] → compute metrics.

**No training is performed.**

---

### Experiment B — Fine-tune Pretrained Model

**Purpose**: Evaluate whether a small amount of VesSAP data can adapt the pretrained model
to the new domain.

**Protocol**:
- Initialise from pretrained micro-CT weights
- Fine-tune on VesSAP patches for **50 epochs**
- Learning rate: 1e-4 (lower than training from scratch, to preserve pretrained features)
- Scheduler: CosineAnnealingLR
- Optimiser: AdamW (weight_decay=1e-5)
- Loss: Dice + BCE (pos_weight=9.0)

---

### Experiment C — Train From Scratch

**Purpose**: Lower-bound reference — what happens if we ignore the pretrained model entirely
and train only on VesSAP data?

**Protocol**:
- Randomly initialise FullAttentionUNet
- Train on VesSAP patches for **100 epochs** (more epochs needed without pretrained init)
- Learning rate: 1e-3
- Same scheduler, optimiser, and loss as Exp B
- Early stopping with patience=15 if loss plateaus

## Analysis

### 1. Significant Domain Gap Analysis

Direct inference (**Exp A: Zero-shot**) on the VesSAP dataset achieves a mean Dice score of **0.54**, compared to the original **MiniVess (2-Photon)** testing domain — a performance drop of **34 percentage points**. 

This substantial gap confirms the sensitivity of our **2.5D Attention U-Net** to imaging modality shifts, which we attribute to two fundamental physical differences:

* **Excitation Physics:** MiniVess (Point-scanning 2-Photon) produces highly localized, sharp signals with high contrast, whereas VesSAP (Light-sheet) involves wide-field plane illumination, which introduces **volumetric scattering** and **striping artifacts**.
* **Signal Continuity:** In our **2.5D architecture**, the Skip-Connection Attention was optimized for the high-SNR, sub-micron textures of live imaging. When presented with the more "diffuse" and lower-resolution voxel intensities of **Ex-vivo VesSAP (ch1)**, the model initially struggled to maintain fine capillary connectivity.

This result justifies the necessity of our **Fine-tuning strategy**, proving that while the model captures vascular topology, its feature extractors require calibration for different fluorescence signal-to-noise ratios (SNR).

### 2. ch0_only Consistently Outperforms max_fusion
Across all three experiments and both folds, the ch0_only strategy achieves higher Dice than
max_fusion (0.540 vs 0.536 in Exp A, 0.718 vs 0.568 in Exp B, 0.713 vs 0.555 in Exp C).
This suggests that the WGA channel (ch0) produces images more similar in appearance to
micro-CT — both are single-channel greyscale images where vessels appear as bright tubular
structures against a dark background. The EB channel (ch1) introduces vessel appearance
characteristics that the model was not trained on, and max_fusion inherits this mismatch.

### 3. Fine-tuning Improves Performance but Advantage over From-Scratch is Modest
Fine-tuning (Exp B) improves mean Dice from 0.540 to 0.718 for ch0_only — a gain of
**+17.8 pp** over direct inference. However, training from scratch (Exp C) achieves nearly
identical performance (0.713), suggesting that with only 1 training sample and 100-121
patches, the pretrained weights do not provide a statistically robust advantage.

This is likely because the domain shift is large enough that the pretrained features require
substantial modification during fine-tuning anyway. With more VesSAP training data, we would
expect Exp B to outperform Exp C more clearly.

### 4. High Variance Between Folds
Fold_1 (test on sample_1) consistently underperforms fold_2 (test on sample_2):

| | fold_1 Dice | fold_2 Dice | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exp B ch0_only | 0.651 | 0.785 | 0.134 |
| Exp C ch0_only | 0.603 | 0.823 | 0.220 |

This high inter-fold variance (up to 0.22 Dice) indicates that the two VesSAP samples differ
substantially in their imaging characteristics. sample_1 appears to be a harder test case —
possibly due to different tissue preparation, staining intensity, or anatomical region.
With only 2 samples, LOO cross-validation cannot reliably estimate generalisation performance.

### 5. Fold_1 Shows Over-Prediction Pattern
In fold_1 across multiple experiments, the optimal threshold is pushed to 0.90 yet Recall
remains extremely high (0.93–0.99) while Precision is low (0.20–0.49). This indicates the
model is over-predicting vessels — outputting high probability values across most of the
volume. This is consistent with a distribution mismatch where the model cannot confidently
distinguish vessel from background in sample_1's imaging conditions.

### 6. clDice Tracks Dice Closely
Centerline Dice (clDice), a topology-aware metric that penalises broken vessel continuity,
closely tracks standard Dice across all experiments. In fold_2 the model achieves
clDice ≈ 0.78–0.79, suggesting that when the model performs well on Dice, it also preserves
vessel topology reasonably well.

---

## Limitations

- **Only 2 VesSAP samples** — LOO cross-validation with 2 samples cannot produce statistically
  reliable estimates. Results are highly sensitive to the specific test sample chosen.
- **1 training sample** — training (Exp B, C) on a single volume with ~100 patches is
  severely data-limited. Both experiments are expected to overfit.
- **No validation set** — with only 1 training sample there is no held-out validation set,
  so early stopping uses training loss only, which does not reliably prevent overfitting.
- **2D model on 3D data** — the 2.5D slice-by-slice approach loses full 3D context compared
  to a native 3D model, which may be more important for LSFM data with high axial resolution.
